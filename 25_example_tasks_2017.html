Coding

General note on the difficulty and amount of work of the tasks in the “Coding” category

The tasks may seem complex when one implements them in other programming languages. However, due to the power and the simplicity of our visual programming language Catrobat used in our Pocket Code app together with our image editing app Pocket Paint, the amount of work to realize these tasks is much smaller, and the apps are much simpler to implement than they would be with other programming languages. See, e.g., how to make a Compass app, from installation of the Pocket Code app on your phone to fully working compass in less than one minute, on https://youtu.be/U7zKDotDFOw or hundreds of other tutorial videos and playlists on our YouTube channel http://catrob.at/youtube – Booyakasha!

1: Introduce yourself with a Catrobat program

In the Pocket Code app, use several speech bubble bricks to tell us a little bit about yourself. Make sure that your program contains only general information about yourself, for example (but not necessarily all of these) your hobbies, music or TV series you like, preferred school subjects, country, mother tongue, which games you like, your pets, your family and/or friends, or opinions you have about important or recent events. Include images or sound bytes that illustrate what your avatar on the screen is telling the user of your app.

Make especially very sure your program does not contain any personal information such as your real name, address, or photos of you, your family, or your friends, neither in the program itself, nor in the description or the name of your program. Upload your program to Catrobat’s sharing platform. When you create an account on Catrobat’s sharing site, please either use OAuth with your Google+ or Facebook account to login, or create a new Catrobat account. Do not use a username for your avatar that allows to identify or contact you, for instance do not use your real name, email address, your birthdate, or the name of an account that you have already used on the web and that would allow other people to identify you in the future (note that photos can also be used to identify persons using face recognition technology as included in popular services such as Facebook or Google Photos). Add an avatar image to your Catrobat account that similarly cannot be tracked to your real name.

If you feel unsure whether you should include certain information, please talk to your parents or other persons which you trust. In case of doubt, do not include the information. After the acceptance of your task, you have the possibility to delete your program from Catrobat’s sharing site by navigating to your “My Profile” page (tap on three dots at the top right on the page) and tap on the white disc with an “x” in it at the top right of the thumbnail of the project.

Your program should use English as its communication language with your user, e.g., in the text of the speech bubbles, but please do not worry if the grammar or pronunciation is not perfect – we will not consider these at all when evaluating your program.

Do not use any abbreviations in your program, and structure it clearly.

Task tags: Visual programming; App creation.

2: Showcase: educational Catrobat game

Create a single-level game with Pocket Code in one of the categories Adventure, Puzzle, Quiz, Action, Racing, Shooter, Simulation, RPG, or Strategy, that is related to a topic from your preferred school subject. Example ideas for your game (but please, use something else!): French coffee shop simulator game (foreign language: French), outdoor real-life running game with acceleration sensors + GPS + timekeeping and spoken output of achieved score (Sports), European politics and war simulation game with Napoleon (Geography/History), blood cells animation and virus fighting game (Biology), famous painters such as as Monet and Picasso inspired art quiz (Art), pop-music sampler riddle game (Music), or Pokémon Go style augmented reality game using GPS + other sensors and the camera (Maths).

Do not use any abbreviations in your program, and structure it clearly. Upload your program to Catrobat’s sharing platform. Create an Android App (apk) out of your program, install it on your phone, and make a screenshot of its launcher icon on your phone’s screen.

Create a screen recording video showing how to play your game and what features it has. Show off! Before doing the screen recording, turn on the option in the developer’s option on your phone that allows to see the finger positions when the screen is touched. If your game uses some of the sensors of your phone, e.g., the inclination sensors, also add a sequence in your video that shows this from an outside perspective (you may need a second person, or a webcam for that part of the recording). At the end of your video, also explain at least one non-trivial feature related to the your game, using a narrated screen recording showing the corresponding parts of your program, in particular the scripts and, in the formula editor, the formulas that you have used.

Use your mother tongue throughout the video, including in Android or in the app, as long as we support your mother tongue (otherwise set your phone to English, but explain in your mother tongue). Your video should have visually appealing intro and end sequences. Create these intro and end scenes with Pocket Code and use your screen recordings to append them at the beginning and end of your video. The video should show your phone in portrait mode, unless in parts where the phone is held in landscape mode. Publish your video on YouTube using an appropriate title and a good description in your mother tongue. Add English language captions to your video through YouTube’s interface for adding captions in various languages.

3: Showcase: Catrobat game using remixing and scenes

Using Pocket Code and Pocket Paint, remix an existing game created with Pocket Code by another user. Use the scene bricks to structure in at least five scenes: Add a new intro scene where the objective of the game is explained, add a tutorial scene for the game, add a completely new “boss” level, and add an end scene to the game. The original game that you remix must be fairly challenging, but you may slightly enhance it if you want. The new, second level of the game must fit the same general theme but cannot be a simple variant of the existing level. Instead, the new level must be of a different genre of a game. The final scene must conclude the game, and allow to restart the game in case the player lost.

Example (this is just an example; chose a totally different game to remix and a different new level to add!): Robin was drunk and has been put into the dungeons by Nottingham’s sheriff. Marian, Robin’s girlfriend, after getting no responses from Robin to her increasingly urgent WhatsApp messages, becomes more and more worried about him. Finally she hears from her girlfriends about his capture, whom they heard from their boyfriends who were out drinking with him in the tavern last night, before he started a fist fight with a group of other drunken ruffians. OMG! However, she loves him and decides to free him. First level: She has to navigate through a maze against the clock. The maze is filled with various delaying or even fatal traps, e.g., a pool party at her best friend’s home, a 50%-off sales offer at a shoe shop that she cannot pass, an ice cream stand that delays her just for a few seconds, a cute cat on the way, etc. A second level could be that the sheriff is very angry about a mixup in the local arts museum. If Marian can help him match up the paintings with the descriptions, he will feel much better and most likely let Robin go out of prison early. The player is shown pictures and must match them with the names of their painters. The end scene could be either a happy end with Robin freed from prison and lots of kisses, or you crying in your girlfriend’s arms and giving up on Robin. What a mess! It’s his own fault, stupid drunkard! See https://share.catrob.at/pocketcode/program/2433 for a very old version of a similar game, at that time still without the possibility to use the scene bricks.

Do not use any abbreviations in your program, and structure it clearly. Once finished, upload your program to Catrobat’s sharing platform. Create an Android App (apk) out of your program, install it on your phone, and make a screenshot of its launcher icon on your phone’s screen. Make sure that you give due credit to the original author of the first level in the description of your remix of the original game. All written or spoken text must be in your mother tongue. If needed, change the language of the original level of the other person’s game.

Create a screen recording video showing how to play your game and what features it has. Show off! Before doing the screen recording, turn on the option in the developer’s option on your phone that allows to see the finger positions when the screen is touched. If your game uses some of the sensors of your phone, e.g., the inclination sensors, also add a sequence in your video that shows this from an outside perspective (you may need a second person, or a webcam for that part of the recording). At the end of your video, also explain at least one non-trivial feature related to the your game, using a narrated screen recording showing the corresponding parts of your program, in particular the scripts and, in the formula editor, the formulas that you have used.

Use your mother tongue throughout the video, including in Android or in the app, as long as we support your mother tongue (otherwise set your phone to English, but explain in your mother tongue). Your video should have visually appealing intro and end sequences. Create these intro and end scenes with Pocket Code and use your screen recordings to append them at the beginning and end of your video. The video should show your phone in portrait mode, unless in parts where the phone is held in landscape mode. Publish your video on YouTube using an appropriate title and a good description in your mother tongue. Add English language captions to your video through YouTube’s interface for adding captions in various languages.

4: Showcase for a featured program: Catrobat game with physics game engine

Create an engaging, visually stunning, high-resolution game using Pocket Code and Pocket Paint that uses, among other bricks, Catrobat’s powerful physics game engine. Example (this is just an example; chose a totally different game!): A wood maze with a metallic ball, where you have to steer the ball through the maze using the inclination and acceleration sensors, avoiding the holes in which the ball can fall. The movement of the ball is 100% natural, using the physics game engine, and the bouncing of the ball on the walls makes the phone slightly vibrate. When the ball falls into a hole, there is a stronger vibration, and the game restarts after a short delay. The score shown once the ball falls into a hole corresponds to the number of holes the player could navigate around. If the player reaches the end of the maze, a success screen is shown. See https://share.catrob.at/pocketcode/program/10445 for a similar project.

At the end of your video, also explain at least one non-trivial feature related to the physics game engine that you have used in your game, using a narrated screen recording showing the corresponding parts of your program, in particular the scripts and, in the formula editor, the formulas that you used related to the physics engine.

Use your mother tongue throughout the video, including in Android or in the app, as long as we support your mother tongue (otherwise set your phone to English, but explain in your mother tongue). 5: Showcase for a featured program: Magic app that uses your phone’s sensors

Create an engaging, visually stunning, high-resolution Magic app using Pocket Code and Pocket Paint that uses, among other bricks and functions, several of your phone’s sensors, as follows: Use your phone as a magic wand that speaks certain spells, depending on the movement of the wand! In it, explain all the features of your magic app and how to move it so that the spells are spoken. Later, whenever the app is not used for 15 seconds or more, only allow spells again if the secret phrase is first spoken into the microphone. 4: YouTube video about how to make backgrounds transparent

Create a screen recording video from your phone (where either your phone, or Pocket Code and Pocket Paint are set to your mother tongue), with spoken explanations in your mother tongue, in which you explain how to erase (= make transparent) the background of Looks from photos and from images downloaded from the Web using Pocket Paint from within Pocket Code.

In particular, in your video explain how one can use Pocket Paint’s Cursor, Crop, Fill, Eraser, Zoom, and Rectangle/Square (this latter one is not obvious, but using two step can be used to erase large areas) tools, possibly in combination, to make a background transparent. Also explain how to use the color tolerance of the Fill tool setting to achieve better results when areas that in principle would be single colored, in fact contain small image parts that are in slightly different colors, e.g., due to antialiasing effects. Explain how to proceed when the foreground object contains holes through which the background should be visible, e.g., text where some letters such as “b” contain holes.

In your video, also show and explain why this is an important skill for using the foreground figure or object in your games, and show an example of a look of an object where the background was first erased with the methods explained in your video, and how the look then was used in a Catrobat program created with Pocket Code.

Before doing the screen recording, turn on the option in the developer’s option on your phone that allows to see the finger positions when the screen is touched.  Your video should have visually appealing intro and end sequences. Create these intro and end scenes with Pocket Code and use your screen recordings to append them at the beginning and end of your video. The video should show your phone in portrait mode. Publish your video on YouTube using an appropriate title and a good description in your mother tongue.
